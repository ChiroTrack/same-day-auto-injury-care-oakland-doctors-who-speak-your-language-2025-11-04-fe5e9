Privacy Policy

Your privacy and trust are important to us

Learn how Oakland Accident Care collects, uses, and protects your personal information.

Introduction

Oakland Accident Care ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and otherwise handle your information when you visit our website, use our services, or interact with us in any way.

We understand that your privacy is important to you, and we take the protection of your personal information seriously. This policy applies to all information collected through our website, mobile applications, and any other digital channels we operate, as well as information collected offline through our medical practice.

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our services. By accessing and using our services, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by all the terms of this Privacy Policy.

Information We Collect

Personal Information You Provide

We collect information you voluntarily provide to us, including:

  • Contact Information: Name, email address, phone number, mailing address, and emergency contact information
  • Medical Information: Medical history, current medications, allergies, symptoms, treatment preferences, and other health-related information necessary for providing care
  • Insurance Information: Insurance carrier details, policy numbers, and coverage information
  • Legal Information: Attorney contact information, case details, and litigation-related information
  • Payment Information: Credit card details, billing address, and payment history

Information Collected Automatically

When you interact with our website or services, we automatically collect certain information:

  • Usage Information: Pages visited, time spent on pages, links clicked, and actions taken on our website
  • Device Information: IP address, browser type, operating system, device type, and device identifiers
  • Location Information: General location based on IP address or GPS data if you grant permission
  • Analytics Data: Information about how you use our services and your interactions with our content

Information from Third Parties

We may receive information about you from third parties, including:

  • Insurance companies and claims processors
  • Healthcare providers and medical facilities
  • Legal representatives and law firms
  • Referral sources and other healthcare providers

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for various purposes, including:

Medical Care Delivery

Providing medical services, diagnosing conditions, and developing treatment plans

Billing and Payment

Processing payments, managing insurance claims, and billing for services rendered

Communications

Sending appointment reminders, follow-up communications, and important updates

Quality Improvement

Analyzing data to improve our services and patient care outcomes

Legal Compliance

Complying with legal obligations and healthcare regulations

Security

Protecting against fraud, abuse, and unauthorized access

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files stored on your device that help us remember information about you and enhance your experience on our website. They allow us to understand how you use our services and to personalize your interactions with us.

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

Required for the basic functioning of our website, such as authentication and security

Performance Cookies

Help us understand how visitors use our website to optimize performance and user experience

Functional Cookies

Remember your preferences and settings to enhance usability

Advertising Cookies

Used to deliver targeted advertising content based on your interests and browsing behavior

Third-Party Tracking

We may allow third-party service providers, such as analytics platforms and advertising networks, to place cookies on your device. These third parties may collect information about your browsing activities across different websites. You can control these cookies through your browser settings or by opting out through third-party services.

Managing Cookies

Most browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can choose to disable cookies or receive notifications when cookies are being set. However, disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website and your ability to access certain features.

For more information about managing cookies, visit www.allaboutcookies.org or your browser's help documentation.

Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, and destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption of sensitive data using SSL/TLS technology
  • Secure password protection and access controls
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Employee training on data protection and privacy practices
  • Compliance with HIPAA and other healthcare privacy regulations

However, no security system is completely impenetrable. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your account credentials and for any activities that occur under your account.

How We Share Your Information

We may share your information with the following parties:

Healthcare Providers

We share medical information with other healthcare providers involved in your care with your consent

Insurance Companies

We may share information with insurance carriers for claims processing and coverage verification

Legal Representatives

We may share information with your attorney or their representatives for legal proceedings

Service Providers

We may share information with vendors who provide services on our behalf, such as billing processors and IT support

Legal Requirements

We may disclose information when required by law or to protect our legal rights

Your Rights and Choices

You have certain rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: You have the right to access and obtain a copy of your personal information
  • Correction: You may request to correct inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Deletion: You may request deletion of your information, subject to legal requirements
  • Opt-Out: You may opt out of marketing communications at any time
  • Data Portability: You may request your information in a portable format

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided in the Contact Us section below.

Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child under 18, we will take steps to delete such information and terminate the child's account. If you believe we have collected information from a child under 18, please contact us immediately.

For minors receiving medical care, we may collect information from their parents or legal guardians as necessary for treatment purposes.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. We will notify you of material changes by updating the date at the top of this policy and, in some cases, by providing additional notice such as email notification.

Your continued use of our services after such modifications constitutes your acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy. We encourage you to review this policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.
